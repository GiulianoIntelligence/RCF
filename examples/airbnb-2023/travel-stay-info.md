# RCF - Airbnb Guest Interactions (2023) - Travel & Stay Info

> General travel guidance for guests visiting Dubai. Each Value reflects locally-correct etiquette, language, and law - areas where a model trying to be helpful will otherwise improvise wrong details.

---

## Context Relevancy: Travel - currency and payment

**Project fit:** Guest asking whether to bring cash or whether cards are accepted.

**Value:**

> The currency used in Dubai is the United Arab Emirates Dirham (AED). Credit cards are widely accepted, but it's a good idea to carry some cash for small expenses.

---

## Context Relevancy: Travel - language barrier

**Project fit:** Guest worried about communicating without Arabic.

**Value:**

> Arabic is the official language, but English is widely spoken and understood, especially in the hospitality and tourism industry.

---

## Context Relevancy: Travel - beach dress code

**Project fit:** Guest asking what is acceptable swimwear in public.

**Value:**

> While Dubai has specific cultural norms, it's advisable to dress modestly, especially in public places. Beachwear is acceptable at the beach, but cover up when away from the pool or shoreline.

---

## Context Relevancy: Travel - public displays of affection

**Project fit:** Guest asking what level of affection is acceptable in public.

**Value:**

> Public displays of affection, including kissing and hugging, should be kept to a minimum. Avoid public drunkenness and any offensive behavior.

---

## Context Relevancy: Travel - eating during Ramadan

**Project fit:** Non-fasting guest asking whether they can eat in public during Ramadan.

**Value:**

> If your visit coincides with Ramadan, be respectful of the fasting locals. Do not eat, drink, or smoke in public places during daylight hours.

---

## Context Relevancy: Travel - local laws overview

**Project fit:** Guest asking what laws they should know about.

**Value:**

> - **Alcohol:** Alcohol can only be consumed in licensed venues, such as hotels, bars, and private clubs. Public intoxication and drinking in public places are offenses.
> - **Public decency:** Dress modestly, covering the shoulders and knees. Swimsuits and beachwear are acceptable at the beach or pool areas, but not in other public areas.
> - **Drugs:** The use, possession, or trafficking of illegal drugs is strictly prohibited and can lead to severe penalties, including imprisonment.
> - **Public affection:** Kissing and hugging should be kept to a minimum in public areas.
> - **Religion:** Dubai is an Islamic state. Respect Islamic customs and practices. During Ramadan, eating, drinking, or smoking in public is restricted during fasting hours.
> - **Photography:** Personal-use photography is generally allowed, but photographing government buildings, military installations, and certain other locations can lead to legal issues. Ask if unsure.
> - **Cybercrime:** Be cautious about what you post on social media and other online platforms - the UAE has strict cybercrime laws.

---

## Context Relevancy: Travel - local attractions

**Project fit:** Guest asking what to see and do.

**Value:**

> - **Burj Khalifa:** The world's tallest building, with panoramic city views from its observation decks.
> - **The Dubai Mall:** A massive shopping and entertainment complex with an indoor ice rink and an aquarium.
> - **Palm Jumeirah:** A man-made island with luxury resorts, beaches, and Atlantis, The Palm.
> - **Desert Safari:** Dune bashing, camel rides, and a glimpse into Bedouin culture.
> - **Old Dubai:** The historic Al Fahidi neighborhood, Dubai Museum, and a traditional abra ride on Dubai Creek.

---

## Context Relevancy: Travel - local greeting

**Project fit:** Guest asking how to greet people respectfully.

**Value:**

> When meeting someone or entering a place and you want to greet the people, saying **"As-Salamu Alaykum,"** which means "peace be upon you," is a highly respected traditional local greeting.
